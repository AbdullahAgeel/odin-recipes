Libyan Couscous

Couscous or as Libyans call it, Cousksi, is a North African dish adored by many who have had the pleasure of putting it in their mouths (pause)

This North African dish originated in Morocco and slowly spread in the North Africa region to neighbouring countries such as Algeria, Libya and Tunisia. Each country added their own twist to the dish. The dish we will be focusing on here is the Libyan version of couscous called 'Cousksi'. Libya added carmelized onions, stew and a touch of spice to the original dish. That is why you will notice that the libyan version has a red colour which is in contract with the Moroccan white version.This dish it is not easy to make or master, Libyan moms learned how to perfect this dish with time and practise. Its recipe is may seem easy due to the low number of ingredients but don't let that fool you!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of durum wheat semolina
  • Lamb, cut into chunks
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 zucchinis, chopped
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • A handful of fresh parsley, chopped
  • salt
  • Black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of coriander
  • Olive oil
  1. Prepare the couscous by sprinkling semolina with water and rolling it into tiny granules. Sieve for uniformity
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Brown the lamb chunks on all sides.
  3. Add onions and garlic, saut√© until translucent.
  4. Add carrots, zucchinis, chickpeas, spices, salt, pepper, and enough water to cover everything. Bring to a boil, then simmer.
  5. Place prepared couscous in a steamer or colander above the simmering stew and steam for about 20 minutes until fluffy.
  6. Serve hot with ladles of stew, garnished with fresh parsley.
